The First Burn - Most Important!

Your candle's first burn sets the stage for its entire lifespan. This initial burn creates what's called the "memory ring" - the width to which your candle will burn for all future uses.

1
Allow 3-4 Hours

Let your candle burn until the wax melts completely across the surface, reaching the edges of the container.

2
Create Full Melt Pool

This prevents tunneling and ensures even burning throughout the candle's life.

3
Be Patient

Soy wax burns slower than paraffin, so it may take longer to achieve the full melt pool.

Wick Trimming Guide

Proper wick maintenance is crucial for optimal candle performance, safety, and longevity. Our wooden wicks require special attention.

1
Trim Before Each Use

Always trim the wick to 1/4 inch before lighting. This prevents excessive flame and smoking.

2
Use Proper Tools

Use wick trimmers or nail clippers. Remove all wick debris from the wax pool.

3
Wooden Wick Care

Break off the charred portion of wooden wicks rather than cutting. This ensures proper burning.

Safety Guidelines

Your safety is our top priority. Follow these essential safety tips when enjoying your candles.

Never Leave Unattended

Always extinguish candles when leaving the room or going to sleep.

Keep Away from Flammables

Maintain at least 12 inches clearance from curtains, books, and other materials.

Pet & Child Safety

Keep candles out of reach of children and pets. Use stable, heat-resistant surfaces.

Avoid Drafts

Place candles away from vents, fans, and open windows to prevent uneven burning.

Cool Before Moving

Allow candles to cool completely before moving or handling the container.

Stop at 1/2 Inch

Discontinue use when 1/2 inch of wax remains to prevent container damage.

Proper Storage

Correct storage maintains your candle's quality and extends its shelf life.

Temperature Control

Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat sources.

Upright Position

Always store candles upright to prevent warping and maintain shape.

Keep Clean

Dust candles regularly with a soft, dry cloth to maintain appearance.

Original Packaging

Keep unused candles in original packaging to protect from dust and damage.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Solutions to common candle problems for optimal performance.

Tunneling

Problem: Candle burns down the center, leaving wax on sides

Solution: Ensure first burn creates full melt pool. Use aluminum foil to reflect heat to edges.

Excessive Smoking

Problem: Candle produces black smoke while burning

Solution: Trim wick to 1/4 inch. Move away from drafts. Extinguish and relight.

Large Flame

Problem: Flame is too large or flickering excessively

Solution: Extinguish candle, trim wick shorter, and relight in draft-free area.

Poor Scent Throw

Problem: Candle doesn't fill room with fragrance

Solution: Ensure full melt pool. Try smaller room. Allow 30 minutes for scent to develop.
